What is an Energy Audit in Delhi?

In the National Capital Region (NCR), high maximum demand charges and stringent pollution control board (DPCC) norms demand extreme operational efficiency. An energy audit in Delhi is a meticulous investigation into how your commercial building, hospital, or factory consumes power from the grid or from localized DG sets, and creating concrete pathways to minimize those drains.

B2B Industrial Solutions stations a rapid-response team of BEE Accredited Energy Auditors for our Delhi-based industrial and corporate clients. By identifying mechanical mismatches in central AC units, compressor leaks, and load anomalies, we systematically lower the specific energy footprint of your facility.

Why Your Delhi Industry Needs It Now

With Delhi continuously enforcing GRAP (Graded Response Action Plan) levels, dependence on pure grid electricity efficiently is more important than ever. Inefficiencies not only bloat your operational costs but push your machinery harder, generating excess heat and maintenance burdens.

  • Crush High Peak Tariffs: Re-align your load profile to avoid expensive peak time (TOD) usage parameters.
  • Reduce Maximum Demand (MD): Mitigate penalty clauses from BSES or Tata Power by optimizing your actual connected load.
  • DPCC Compliance: Run your utility plants more cleanly, lowering overall emissions and thermal output.
  • Equipment Longevity: Reducing harmonics and optimizing VFDs expands the lifespan of expensive imported machinery.

Yes. From the heavy manufacturing zones of Bawana and Narela to commercial setups in Connaught Place and Okhla Phase 1/2/3, we service all of Delhi NCR.

What components of our building will you evaluate during the audit?

Our BEE certified auditors inspect all high-draw systems: Centralized HVAC (chillers, pumps, cooling towers), air compressors, industrial boilers, lighting layouts, and primary power distribution infrastructure.

How often should a Delhi-based factory conduct an Energy Audit?

For continuous process industries, an annual audit is recommended. For general manufacturing and commercial buildings, a comprehensive audit every 2-3 years ensures peak efficiency and compliance.
