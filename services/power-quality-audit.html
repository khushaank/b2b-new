What is a Power Quality Audit?

A Power Quality Audit explores the raw “cleanliness” of the electrical supply entering and circulating through your facility. In industrial setups teeming with complex VFDs (Variable Frequency Drives), soft starters, LED grids, and computer networks, the electrical current waveform gets severely distorted. A Power Quality (PQ) audit identifies voltage sags, swells, transients, and dangerous harmonic distortions (THD-V and THD-I) that degrade machinery, cause unexplained trips, and inflate utility billing.

B2B Industrial Solutions utilizes Class-A power quality analyzers to capture micro-second level anomalies. We mathematically diagnose your electrical waveform and prescribe dedicated active or passive filters to clean up your grid.

Why Your Industry Needs a Power Quality Audit Now

Poor power quality is a silent destroyer of highly sensitive imported capital electronics (like CNC controllers and Robotics).

  • Zero Unexplained Downtime: Eliminate nuisance tripping of drives and breakers caused by transient voltage spikes or high harmonic resonance.
  • Avoid Dis-com Penalties: High harmonic injection into the state grid can result in severe financial penalties or even forced disconnections by local regulatory boards.
  • Boost Equipment Lifespan: High 3rd and 5th order harmonics create severe physical overheating in transformers, bearings, and neutral cables, severely reducing their operational lifespan.

Relevant Indian & International Standards

We analyze against strict global harmonic compliance standards:

  • IEEE-519: The internationally recognized standard for harmonic limits in electrical power systems.
  • IEC 61000: Electromagnetic compatibility limits for power supply networks.
  • CEA Regulations: Central Electricity Authority mandates on maintaining power factor and minimizing harmonic injection.

Our Proven 7-Step PQ Process

1. Baseline Network Review: Modeling the single-line diagram and identifying major non-linear load points (inverters, furnaces, UPS systems).
2. Instrument Deployment: Connecting Class-A loggers directly at the Point of Common Coupling (PCC) and internal sub-panels.
3. Multi-Cycle Data Logging: Recording all variations (voltage, current, frequency, THD, unbalance) across continuous 24 to 72 hour production cycles.
4. Transient Analytics: Filtering through captured data to pinpoint exact moments of sags, swells, or switching transients causing machinery faults.
5. Mathematical Sizing: Calculating the exact kVAR for capacitor banks and the tuned frequencies required for active or passive harmonic filters.
6. PQ Reporting: Delivering the compliance report structured entirely against IEEE-519 parameters.
7. Direct Hardware Solutions: Designing, installing, and commissioning tuned APFC panels and active harmonic filters through our turnkey project wing.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What are Harmonics?

Harmonics are unwanted electrical frequencies generated by non-linear loads (like LEDs, computers, and variable speed motors). They distort the normal 50Hz sine wave, causing excessive heat in panels, transformer degradation, and circuit tripping.

2. Will this fix my poor Power Factor?

Yes. Often, traditional capacitor banks fail or blow fuses because they are exposed to heavy harmonic resonance. A PQ audit identifies the frequencies, so we can install de-tuned or active power factor correction (APFC) systems to securely fix the power factor.
