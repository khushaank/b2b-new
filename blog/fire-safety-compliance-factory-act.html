Factory Act Fire Safety Compliance Checklist

Use this factory fire safety checklist to improve compliance readiness under the Factory Act, from documentation to mock drill execution.

January 06, 2026
7 min read

Fire incidents in industrial facilities can create long operational shutdowns, compliance penalties, and serious risk to life. A structured checklist under the Factory Act helps teams avoid blind spots before inspections and emergencies.

1) Documentation readiness

  • Latest fire load calculations and layout updates.
  • Valid certificates for critical fire safety equipment.
  • AMC and maintenance records for hydrant and pump systems.
  • Inspection observations with closure evidence.

2) Active fire system checks

  • Hydrant pressure test and performance records.
  • Sprinkler coverage verification and valve health checks.
  • Alarm panel, detector, and annunciation functionality tests.
  • Availability and tagging of extinguishers by risk class.

3) Passive protection verification

  • Integrity of fire-rated doors and compartmentation points.
  • Sealing of cable penetrations and shaft openings.
  • Clear and compliant emergency exit signage.

4) Emergency preparedness

  • Site-specific emergency response plan in current format.
  • Role matrix for fire wardens and shift response teams.
  • Mock drill records with observations and corrective actions.
  • Training logs for staff, contractors, and visitors.

Compliance is strongest when technical systems and people readiness are reviewed together, not in separate silos.
