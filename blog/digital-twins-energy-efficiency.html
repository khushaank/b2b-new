Digital Twins for Energy Efficiency in Manufacturing

Digital twins help teams test energy scenarios before changing equipment settings on the shop floor.

June 10, 2026
5 min read
B2B Industrial Solutions
  1. Home
  2. /
  3. Blog
  4. /
  5. Digital Twins

A digital twin is a working model of a system that mirrors real operating data. For energy teams, it can simplify decisions around HVAC schedules, compressed air pressure, boiler loads, and production-linked energy use.

Energy use cases

  • Compare expected and actual consumption.
  • Simulate set-point changes before implementation.
  • Identify abnormal baseloads during non-production hours.
  • Track specific energy consumption by product or line.

Data requirements

Useful twins need clean meter data, production data, operating schedules, and asset information. Without these, the model becomes a presentation layer rather than a decision tool.

The best digital twin projects begin with one question: what decision should this model improve?

Practical outcome

When tied to energy audits, digital twins can help verify savings and maintain improvements after recommendations are implemented.

Start with a minimum viable twin

Choose one decision with measurable value, such as chiller sequencing, compressed-air pressure, furnace scheduling, or pump operation. Model only the variables needed to improve that decision. A smaller model is easier to calibrate, explain, and maintain than a plant-wide replica built before anyone agrees how it will be used.

Validate before operators trust it

  • Compare predicted and actual behaviour across normal load, low load, and abnormal conditions.
  • Document sensor quality, assumptions, exclusions, and the range where the model is reliable.
  • Test recommendations with operators and record why a suggestion was accepted or rejected.
  • Recalibrate after equipment, controls, products, or operating schedules change.

The practical test is simple: does the model help someone make a faster, safer, or more efficient decision? If it only produces a dashboard, the project has digitised visibility but not improved performance.
