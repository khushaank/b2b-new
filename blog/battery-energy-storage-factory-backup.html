Battery Energy Storage for Factory Backup and Peak Shaving

BESS projects can support resilience, demand control, and renewable integration when sized correctly.

May 18, 2026
6 min read
B2B Industrial Solutions

Battery Energy Storage Systems are becoming relevant for factories that face peak demand charges, power quality concerns, DG dependence, or solar intermittency.

Where BESS helps

  • Peak shaving during high-demand windows.
  • Backup for selected critical loads.
  • Solar energy shifting from generation hours to usage hours.
  • Reducing DG runtime in specific operating cases.

What to assess first

Plants should review load curves, demand charges, outage history, critical load lists, fire safety provisions, and available electrical space before selecting a battery size.

A battery system should be sized from load data, not from assumptions about total connected load.

Safety and compliance

Battery rooms and containers need proper ventilation, fire detection, isolation, access control, and maintenance planning.

Build the business case from interval data

Use the plant load profile to separate short peaks, sustained demand, essential backup loads, and energy that can be shifted. Then test the intended operating strategy: backup, peak shaving, solar self-consumption, or a combination. Each strategy asks the battery to cycle differently, so the useful capacity and expected life cannot be judged from the nameplate rating alone.

Questions to settle before procurement

  • Which loads must remain online, and for exactly how long?
  • What reserve will be maintained for an outage after normal daily dispatch?
  • How will degradation, ambient temperature, auxiliary consumption, and future expansion be modelled?
  • Who owns alarms, emergency response, preventive maintenance, and end-of-life planning?

A credible proposal should show the assumptions behind savings and resilience separately. That makes it possible to compare vendors without treating every promised benefit as if it can occur at the same time.
