CPCB Emission Monitoring: What Factories Should Track

Emission compliance becomes easier when monitoring, testing, and documentation work together.

May 29, 2026
5 min read
B2B Industrial Solutions

Factories using boilers, DG sets, process stacks, or emission control equipment need a clear monitoring plan. Waiting for inspection season creates documentation pressure and avoidable compliance risk.

What to track

  • Stack test reports and sampling dates.
  • Fuel type, consumption, and operating hours.
  • Emission control equipment health and maintenance logs.
  • Calibration records for monitoring instruments.
  • OCEMS uptime and data availability where applicable.

Operational controls

Compliance teams should align plant operations, maintenance, and EHS teams so emission sources are operated within approved conditions.

Most compliance gaps are documentation gaps first and technical gaps second.

Review rhythm

A monthly review of emission records helps identify missing reports, equipment issues, and renewal risks before they become urgent.
